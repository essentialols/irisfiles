Frequently Asked Questions

WebM is a subset of the Matroska (MKV) format, but MKV offers broader codec support and features like multiple subtitle tracks, chapter markers, and attachments. Converting to MKV gives you access to the full Matroska feature set while maintaining compatibility with most desktop media players.

IrisFiles uses FFmpeg.wasm, a WebAssembly build of FFmpeg that runs in your browser. Since WebM is a Matroska subset, the conversion re-muxes the streams into a full MKV container. No server-side processing occurs.

Files must be under 100MB and 10 minutes long. For larger videos, use a desktop tool like MKVToolNix, which can remux WebM to MKV almost instantly.
