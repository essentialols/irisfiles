Frequently Asked Questions

MKV (Matroska) is an open-standard container that supports virtually unlimited audio, video, and subtitle tracks in a single file. It is the preferred format for archiving video with multiple languages, commentary tracks, or chapter markers.

IrisFiles runs FFmpeg.wasm, a WebAssembly build of FFmpeg, directly in your browser. It reads the MP4 container and re-muxes the video and audio streams into an MKV container. No data leaves your device.

Files must be under 100MB and 10 minutes long. Browser memory constraints make larger conversions impractical. For bigger files, use a desktop tool like HandBrake, VLC, or MKVToolNix.

When the codec is compatible, the conversion re-muxes the streams without re-encoding, preserving original quality. If re-encoding is needed, some quality difference may occur, though it is generally minimal.
