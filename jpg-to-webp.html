Frequently Asked Questions

WebP produces smaller files than JPG at the same visual quality and is supported by all modern browsers. Switching to WebP can significantly reduce page load times and bandwidth usage.

All major browsers have supported WebP since 2020, including Safari 14+, Chrome, Firefox, and Edge. For older browsers you can fall back to JPG if needed.

WebP files are typically 25-35% smaller than equivalent JPG files at the same perceived quality. The exact savings depend on the image content and chosen quality setting.
