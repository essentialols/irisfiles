Frequently Asked Questions

IrisFiles can handle videos up to 60 seconds. The converter stream-encodes frames one at a time, so it uses minimal memory regardless of video length. On low-memory devices, resolution and frame rate are automatically reduced.

IrisFiles uses your browser's built-in video decoder. MP4 (H.264) works everywhere. WebM works in Chrome, Firefox, and Edge. MOV works in Safari and Chrome. The exact formats depend on your browser and operating system.

Your browser's video element decodes the video natively. IrisFiles captures frames one at a time to a Canvas element, quantizes colors to a 256-color palette, encodes each frame, then discards it. The entire process runs on your device with no network requests. Only 2 frames are ever held in memory at once.

GIF is an inherently large format because each frame stores full pixel data with limited compression. To reduce size: lower the width (320px is often sufficient), reduce FPS to 8, and trim the duration. A 5-second 320px GIF at 8fps will be much smaller than a 30-second 640px GIF at 15fps.
