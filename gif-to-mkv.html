Frequently Asked Questions

MKV (Matroska) is a flexible, open-standard container that supports virtually any video codec, audio codec, and subtitle format. It is the preferred format for media libraries, Plex, Kodi, and other home theater software. Converting GIF to MKV gives you a versatile video file that integrates well with media management tools.

Both are container formats. MP4 has broader device support (phones, smart TVs, game consoles), while MKV supports more codecs, multiple audio tracks, subtitles, and chapter markers. MKV is preferred for archiving and media servers; MP4 is better for sharing and streaming.

IrisFiles uses FFmpeg.wasm, a WebAssembly build of FFmpeg, to decode your animated GIF and encode it as video in an MKV container. Everything runs in your browser. No files are uploaded to any server, and the process works offline after the initial library download.

Yes. Your GIF file never leaves your browser. IrisFiles downloads the FFmpeg.wasm library (~10MB compressed) once from a CDN, then all processing happens locally on your device. No file data is ever sent over the network.
