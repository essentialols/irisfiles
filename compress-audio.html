Frequently Asked Questions

IrisFiles re-encodes your audio to MP3 using FFmpeg.wasm with a configurable bitrate. Lower bitrates produce smaller files with some quality loss. High (192kbps) sounds nearly identical to the original. Medium (128kbps) is a good balance. Low (64kbps) gives maximum compression for voice recordings or podcasts.

High (192kbps) is best for music where quality matters. Medium (128kbps) works well for most uses including sharing online. Low (64kbps) is suitable for voice recordings, podcasts, or when file size is the top priority.

You can compress MP3, WAV, OGG, FLAC, M4A, and AAC files. The output is always MP3 for maximum compatibility across devices and platforms.
