Frequently Asked Questions

PNG is a lossless format, meaning it preserves every pixel exactly as rendered. This makes it ideal for documents with sharp text, diagrams, or screenshots where JPG compression artifacts would be noticeable. The tradeoff is larger file sizes compared to JPG.

For PNG output, the quality slider controls the rendering resolution of the PDF pages before they are saved as PNG. Higher values produce sharper, more detailed images. The default of 100% gives the best results. Since PNG is lossless, there are no compression artifacts at any setting.
