Frequently Asked Questions

WebM is an open, royalty-free video format designed for the web. It produces files that are 90-95% smaller than GIFs while supporting transparency (via VP9 alpha channel), full color depth, and native HTML5 video playback in all modern browsers.

Yes. WebM with VP9 encoding supports an alpha channel for transparency, making it an excellent replacement for transparent GIFs. The resulting file will be vastly smaller while maintaining transparent areas.

IrisFiles uses FFmpeg.wasm, a WebAssembly build of FFmpeg, to decode your animated GIF frame by frame and re-encode the frames as VP8 video in a WebM container. The entire process runs locally in your browser with no server involved.

Yes. Browser-based conversion supports GIF files up to 100MB. For very large GIFs, the conversion may take a minute or two depending on your device. For files larger than 100MB, consider a desktop tool like FFmpeg or HandBrake.
