Frequently Asked Questions

AVI is a legacy format with limited support for modern features. MKV (Matroska) is a modern, open-standard container that supports multiple audio tracks, subtitles, chapters, and a wide range of codecs. It is the preferred format for media servers like Plex and Kodi.

IrisFiles uses FFmpeg.wasm, a WebAssembly build of FFmpeg that runs entirely in your browser. It reads the AVI container, decodes the streams, and re-muxes them into an MKV file. No data leaves your device at any point.

Files must be under 100MB and 10 minutes long. AVI files can be quite large due to older compression methods. For bigger files, use a desktop tool like MKVToolNix (which can remux without re-encoding) or HandBrake.

When the AVI codecs are compatible with the MKV container, the conversion re-muxes without re-encoding, preserving original quality. If re-encoding is required, quality loss is minimal with appropriate settings.
