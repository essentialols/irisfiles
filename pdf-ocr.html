Frequently Asked Questions

IrisFiles first tries to extract embedded text from your PDF using PDF.js. For pages that contain only scanned images, it renders them at 200 DPI, preprocesses them for clarity, and runs Tesseract OCR entirely in your browser using WebAssembly. No data is ever sent to a server.

IrisFiles supports 15 languages: English, German, French, Spanish, Italian, Portuguese, Dutch, Polish, Russian, Japanese, Chinese (Simplified and Traditional), Korean, Arabic, and Hindi. Language models are downloaded on first use and cached for future sessions.

No. IrisFiles processes everything in your browser using WebAssembly. Your PDF files never leave your device. This makes it safe even for confidential or sensitive documents.

The first time you use OCR, IrisFiles downloads the language model (typically 2-5 MB). This is cached in your browser, so subsequent OCR runs start much faster. The OCR engine itself also needs to initialize its WebAssembly runtime on first use.

IrisFiles automatically detects pages with embedded text and extracts it directly, without running OCR. Only pages that lack text content (scanned images) go through the OCR pipeline. This hybrid approach is faster and more accurate.
