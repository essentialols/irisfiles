Frequently Asked Questions

AVI files are often large and not suitable for web use. WebM is an open, royalty-free format designed for the web, offering smaller file sizes with VP8/VP9 compression. Converting AVI to WebM makes your videos ready for HTML5 embedding and web publishing.

IrisFiles uses FFmpeg.wasm, a WebAssembly build of FFmpeg that runs in your browser. It decodes the video and audio from your AVI file and re-encodes them as VP8 video and Vorbis audio in a WebM container. No server is involved.

Files must be under 100MB and 10 minutes in duration. AVI files tend to be large due to minimal compression, so many AVI files may exceed the 100MB limit. For larger files, use HandBrake or VLC.
