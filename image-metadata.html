Frequently Asked Questions

Photos can contain EXIF metadata including GPS coordinates (your exact location), camera make and model, lens information, date and time, exposure settings (ISO, aperture, shutter speed), software used, and descriptive fields like artist and copyright. This data is embedded by cameras and phones automatically.

Yes, for JPEG images. IrisFiles uses piexifjs to modify only the metadata bytes in the file, never re-encoding the pixel data. This means zero quality loss. For non-JPEG formats (PNG, WebP, etc.), metadata can be viewed but stripping requires Canvas re-encoding.

GPS coordinates in photo metadata reveal the exact location where the photo was taken. When you share photos online, anyone can extract this data and determine your home address, workplace, or travel patterns. Removing GPS data before sharing protects your privacy and safety.
