MKV (Matroska) is an open-standard container that supports multiple audio tracks, subtitles, and chapter markers. It is widely supported by VLC, Plex, Kodi, and other cross-platform media players, making it a great choice for archiving MOV files outside the Apple ecosystem.

IrisFiles uses FFmpeg.wasm, a WebAssembly version of FFmpeg. It reads the QuickTime MOV container, extracts the video and audio streams, and packages them into an MKV file. Everything runs locally in your browser with no server involvement.
