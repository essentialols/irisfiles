Frequently Asked Questions

MOV is Apple's QuickTime format, ideal for macOS and iOS workflows. It supports ProRes and other high-quality codecs preferred by video editors like Final Cut Pro, iMovie, and Motion. Converting GIF to MOV lets you use animated content in professional Apple-based editing pipelines.

Yes. MOV is the native format for Apple's video editing tools. The converted file can be imported directly into Final Cut Pro, iMovie, or any QuickTime-compatible application for further editing or compositing.

IrisFiles uses FFmpeg.wasm, a WebAssembly build of FFmpeg, running entirely in your browser. It reads your GIF, decodes each frame, and encodes them into a MOV container. No data is sent to any server. Your files stay on your device.

The browser-based converter supports GIF files up to 100MB. Conversion speed depends on the GIF's resolution and frame count. For very large or complex GIFs, a desktop tool like FFmpeg or HandBrake may be more suitable.
