Frequently Asked Questions

IrisFiles uses FFmpeg.wasm to re-encode your video with adjusted frame timing. For slow motion, each frame is displayed longer (e.g., 2x longer for 0.5x speed). For time lapse, frames are displayed faster. The output is always MP4 (H.264) for maximum compatibility.

The video is re-encoded at high quality (CRF 23) so visual quality stays very close to the original. Slowing down video below the original frame rate may appear less smooth since no new frames are generated. For best slow-motion results, start with high frame rate source footage (60fps or higher).

For moderate speed changes (0.125x to 4x), audio is pitch-corrected and kept by default. For extreme time lapses (8x and 16x), audio is dropped automatically since it becomes unintelligible. You can also manually choose to drop audio using the checkbox.
