Frequently Asked Questions

WebM is designed for web delivery and is natively supported by all modern browsers. If you have MKV files that you want to embed on a website or share online, converting to WebM ensures universal browser playback without plugins.

IrisFiles uses FFmpeg.wasm, a WebAssembly version of FFmpeg. If the MKV already contains VP8/VP9 video and Vorbis/Opus audio, the streams are re-muxed into a WebM container without re-encoding. Otherwise, the streams are transcoded to WebM-compatible codecs.

Files must be under 100MB and 10 minutes. MKV files can be large, especially for HD content. For bigger files, use a desktop tool like HandBrake or FFmpeg on the command line.

Yes. All processing happens in your browser. Your video files are never uploaded to any server. The FFmpeg.wasm library is downloaded once from a CDN and cached locally.
