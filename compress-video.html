Frequently Asked Questions

IrisFiles re-encodes your video using H.264 with a higher CRF (Constant Rate Factor) value, which tells the encoder to use more aggressive compression. You can also downscale the resolution, which dramatically reduces file size. The output is always MP4.

High quality gives minimal visual difference with moderate compression. Medium is a good balance for sharing online or via email. Low produces significantly smaller files but with noticeable quality reduction. For most uses, Medium with 720p resolution gives the best size-to-quality ratio.

Video re-encoding is computationally intensive. IrisFiles runs FFmpeg.wasm entirely in your browser without GPU acceleration, so it relies on your CPU. Expect processing to take roughly 2-5x the video duration. The benefit is that your files never leave your device.
