Frequently Asked Questions

AVI (Audio Video Interleave) is a legacy container format from Microsoft that remains widely supported by older media players, video editing software, and embedded systems. Some hardware players and industrial equipment only accept AVI files.

IrisFiles uses FFmpeg.wasm, a WebAssembly port of FFmpeg that runs entirely in your browser. It decodes the H.264 video and AAC audio from your MP4 file and re-muxes or re-encodes them into an AVI container. No server is involved at any point.

Browser-based conversion supports files up to 100MB and 10 minutes in duration. For larger files, consider using a desktop application like HandBrake or VLC.
