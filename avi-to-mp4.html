Frequently Asked Questions

ConvertFast uses FFmpeg.wasm, a WebAssembly build of FFmpeg that runs entirely in your browser. It re-encodes the video using H.264 and AAC codecs to produce a standard MP4 file. No server is involved.

Video transcoding is computationally intensive. Unlike simple format changes, AVI to MP4 requires fully decoding and re-encoding the video. The browser runs this on your CPU without GPU acceleration, which is slower than native desktop tools. Expect roughly 2-5x the video duration for processing.
