Frequently Asked Questions

EXIF (Exchangeable Image File Format) is metadata embedded in photos by cameras and phones. It can include GPS coordinates, camera model, date and time, lens settings, and even your device's serial number. Sharing photos with EXIF data can reveal your location and other personal information.

ConvertFast re-encodes your image through the browser's Canvas API at maximum quality. This process naturally drops all metadata because Canvas only preserves pixel data. PNG images stay as PNG (lossless), while all other formats are re-encoded as JPEG at quality 1.0.

The quality impact is minimal. JPEG images are re-encoded at the maximum quality setting (1.0), so any visual difference is negligible. PNG images are re-encoded losslessly, so there is zero quality loss.
